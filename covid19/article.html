Why does the virus have different reactions towards different age demographics?

In 2020, a sustained resurgence of the coronavirus disease (COVID-19) surfaced in the United States. Throughout the epidemic, a severe increase of mortality occurred due to the affects the disease had on an individual. When coronavirus enters the body, it goes towards the healthy cells and uses its infectious machinery to make those cells a virus particle. When a cell becomes infected by the virus, it begins to break open, which leads the cells to die and causes a transfer of the virus particle to infect more cells. The reason why COVID-19 affects certain people is because it is connected to inflammation in two ways. Every individual has a different immune response which is why the virus reacts evil towards people of higher levels of inflammation.

INTERVIEW WITH DOCTOR BRIAN HUMMEL

Last week, I interviewed Doctor Brian Hummel who is based in Charlotte, North Carolina. We discussed over zoom about how COVID-19 has affected his hospital. Hummel explains how the pandemic transformed every hospital across the country. Changes have been made in his hospital to reduce spread of infections and to prevent outbreaks of COVID-19 and other infectious diseases that can come this way.In many ways the pandemic highlighted what it means to work together. Doctor Hummel said collaboration and advancing medical ideas on how to corporate the necessities was how they would work together in the hospital during rapid cases of the virus. He began to describe the hardships of communicating with his colleagues with masks and body covers to make sure they would not get infected by the disease. The pandemic brought light to many issues such as racial and socio-economic problems in care within their community.

I have learned that rapid new technology is being released within healthcare and it is expected to bring huge transformations that will change people's perspectives on hospitals. The pandemic has brought light by creating new changes within hospitals all over the world.
